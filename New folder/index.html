Comics are not people. We like talking about death, people like to forget about it. Strange things keep us up at night. Like why cows on milk cartons look so happy? Why are broilers on chicken packets wearing chef hats? If all the CCDs are empty, why are they opening new ones? People don't think about these things, the horrors right in front of our eyes. They can't afford to. They have to reach work the next day. It's a strange barter. They do all the work, we do all the obsessing. Walk into a restaurant and you see how full the tables are and if the coffee is smelling nice. But he comic sees the guy trying very hard to impress his date, the parents conveniently ignoring their rampaging progeny, the staff fighting in the corner and the general incidence of obesity. In a crowded mall, you make a beeline for your favourite brand while ducking the credit card salesmen. The comic notices how sweaty the guy in the takeaway kitchen is, the rats nibbling on a cake that has fallen down, the kid wiping his hands on his father's shorts, how the store staff are trying not to listen to Kenny G for the 500th time and how everyone looks generally dead inside. #LifeinFinland

I'm running around aimlessly, trying not to get caught by the shadows, that are getting closer and closer. My strength is gone and it's just too dark. A cold hand catches my arm and soon I'm embraced by this cruel man, Fear is his name. He makes me stumble and fall to my knees. His hands too strong to resist. I hear the steps coming closer, I know these steps too well. It's the steps of the man that I'm so desperately trying to escape from and yet he always seems to know where to find me. He's not my lover, nor my fianc√©. He's a man, who wants to imprison me and put me in his cage. I shiver when he crouches over me, his cold breath brushing my cheek as he whispers to my ear: "Here you are, my darling. I've been waiting for you." His strong arms lift me effortlessly from the ground. There's absolutely no way to escape. His ice cold eyes are locked to mine and his lips are slightly curved upwards, but the smile is not a gentle one. It's telling me to give up the resistance. Tears are starting run down my cheeks as he walks to his horse with a strong determination. The horse, blackest of them all, called Despair takes us back to the castle. I close my eyes with a sigh. I don't want this. The man holds my chin with his freezing cold hand, forcing me to look at him. His face is getting closer to mine but I can't look away. The gesture seemingly sweet has fragments of terror and torment. He locks his lips with mine as a sign of victory. "You're mine, darling, when will you realise that?" These words would delight me under any other circumstances, but coming from him, it only brings me death. This man is called Loneliness. #LifeinFinland
